Instructions

1. Evaluate the SiteStep 1

Navigate through visitjulian.com on both mobile (primary focus) and desktop (secondary).

Guiding Questions
  • Clarity: Is the website's purpose and content clear?
  • Navigation: Is it easy to find what you're looking for?
  • Readability: Is the text easy to read and understand?
  • Accessibility: Is the website accessible to users with disabilities?
  • Visual Hierarchy: Is information presented in a clear and organized way?
  • Consistency: Is the design consistent throughout the website?
  • Mobile Responsiveness: Does the website work well on different devices?
Pro Tip: A given error might fall under more than one heuristic. Choose the primary heuristic it violates most directly, and distribute your findings so you have at least one violation for each of the 10 heuristics.
2. Record Your FindingsStep 2
For each violation, document:
  • Screenshot showing the issue (annotate with arrows/circles where possible)
  • Heuristic violated (number + name, e.g., "Heuristic #1: Visibility of System Status")
  • Description of what's wrong and why it violates this heuristic
  • User scenario: Describe a realistic situation where this violation causes problems
  • Severity rating (1‚Äì4) with brief justification (see Severity Ratings below)
  • AI enhancement opportunity (Optional): How AI could help address this issue

Format: Notes can be rough but should clearly identify the heuristic, violation, and severity.

Nielsen's 10 Usability Heuristics

Quick ReferenceEssential
# Heuristic In Brief
1 Visibility of System Status Keep users informed about what's happening through timely feedback
2 Match Between System and Real World Use familiar language, concepts, and conventions that users understand
3 User Control and Freedom Provide ways to undo/redo actions and exit unwanted states easily
4 Consistency and Standards Follow platform conventions and be consistent within your interface
5 Error Prevention Prevent problems before they occur through good design
6 Recognition Rather Than Recall Make objects, actions, and options visible; don't make users remember
7 Flexibility and Efficiency of Use Provide shortcuts and ways to customize for experienced users
8 Aesthetic and Minimalist Design Remove irrelevant or rarely needed information; keep it clean
9 Help Users Recognize, Diagnose, and Recover from Errors Error messages should be clear, indicate the problem, and suggest solutions
10 Help and Documentation Provide easy-to-search help that focuses on user tasks

Testing Guide

How to Test ThoroughlyImportant
Try Full Functionality
  • Search functions
  • Payment flows
  • Forms (booking, contact, etc.)
  • Interactive maps
  • Navigation menus
  • Filters and sorting
  • Any booking or reservation systems
Test Error States
  • Invalid credit card numbers (use test numbers like 4111 1111 1111 1111)
  • Nonsense search terms
  • Incomplete forms (submit without filling required fields)
  • Invalid email formats
  • Out-of-range dates or values
Payment Testing: Test payment flows but stop before actually submitting payment. Most sites let you fill out payment forms and see validation without processing the transaction.
Consider AI Opportunities (Optional)

Note where AI could enhance the experience:

  • Smarter search with natural language understanding
  • Personalized recommendations
  • Chatbot assistance for common questions
  • Error prediction and prevention
  • Accessibility improvements

Severity Ratings

Understanding Severity LevelsCritical
Rating Level Description Example
1 Cosmetic Fix if time permits; doesn't affect usability Slightly inconsistent font size that users won't notice
2 Minor Low priority; causes minor inconvenience Unclear button label that users figure out after a moment
3 Major High priority; causes significant problems Broken navigation that makes finding content very difficult
4 Catastrophic Must fix; prevents task completion or causes data loss Payment form that doesn't work; search that never returns results
How to Decide

Consider these questions:

  • How many users will be affected?
  • How often will users encounter this problem?
  • How severe is the impact on the user experience?
  • Can users recover or work around the problem easily?
  • Does it prevent task completion?
Normal Distribution: Typically, you'll find more minor violations (1-2) than severe ones (3-4)‚Äîthat's normal for a functioning website. If everything seems catastrophic, you might be overestimating severity.

AI Use Guidelines

How to Use AI EthicallyOptional

You may optionally use AI tools to support your evaluation process, but AI should augment (not replace) your critical thinking.

‚úÖ Permitted AI Uses

Brainstorming and Ideation

  • Ask AI to suggest additional user scenarios to test
  • Request AI to identify common usability patterns you might have missed
  • Use AI to help articulate why something feels "off"

Analysis Enhancement

  • Use AI to help categorize which heuristic a violation falls under
  • Ask AI for alternative perspectives on severity ratings
  • Request AI to suggest how similar sites handle the same functionality

Documentation Improvement

  • Use AI to help clarify your descriptions
  • Ask AI to suggest better ways to phrase your findings
‚ùå Prohibited AI Uses
Do NOT:
  • Upload screenshots to AI and ask it to "do the heuristic evaluation for you"
  • Copy AI-generated evaluations without your own analysis
  • Let AI substitute for your critical thinking and observation
  • Use AI to generate fake violations you didn't find
Documentation Requirement

If you use AI tools, include an "AI Use Statement" at the end of your submission that includes:

  • Which AI tool(s) you used
  • How you used them (be specific)
  • Which findings were AI-assisted vs. independently discovered
  • How AI changed your evaluation process
Remember: Using AI appropriately and documenting it transparently is encouraged. You will only be penalized for dishonest use or failing to document AI assistance.

Tips for Success

How to Do Well on This AssignmentPro Tips

Finding Violations

  • Start broad, then narrow: First explore the whole site to get familiar, then focus on specific features and user flows
  • Test like a real user: Don't just click around randomly‚Äîthink of realistic tasks someone would try to accomplish
  • Try to break things: Intentionally enter invalid data, skip steps, use edge cases
  • Check both platforms: Some violations only appear on mobile or desktop
  • Look for patterns: Inconsistencies across pages often reveal violations

Taking Screenshots

  • Mobile: iPhone (Side + Volume Up), Android (Power + Volume Down)
  • Desktop: Or use browser dev tools to simulate mobile (Chrome: F12 ‚Üí Toggle device toolbar)
  • Annotate clearly: Use arrows, circles, highlights to point out the specific problem
  • Keep it simple: Basic annotation tools (Preview on Mac, Paint on Windows) work fine
  • Multiple shots: For complex issues, show the sequence of steps that reveals the problem

Writing Effective Descriptions

  • Be specific: "Button label unclear" is vague; "Book Now button labeled 'Continue' causes confusion about what happens next" is specific
  • Connect to heuristics: Don't just describe the problem‚Äîexplain which principle it violates and why
  • Think user impact: Always explain how this affects real users trying to accomplish real goals
  • Avoid personal opinions: Focus on usability impact, not "I don't like this design"

Choosing Severity

  • Consider frequency: How often will users encounter this?
  • Consider impact: Can users recover? Does it prevent task completion?
  • Be realistic: Not everything is catastrophic‚Äîsave severity 4 for truly broken functionality
  • Justify your choice: Briefly explain why you chose that particular rating

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • ‚ùå Listing the same violation under multiple heuristics
  • ‚ùå Describing personal preferences as usability violations
  • ‚ùå Screenshots without annotation showing what the problem is
  • ‚ùå Vague descriptions that don't explain the actual problem
  • ‚ùå Missing user scenarios that show real-world impact
  • ‚ùå Severity ratings without justification
  • ‚ùå Waiting until the last minute (give yourself time!)
